MAE 3230 — Fluid Mechanics

Chainsaw Engine Fluid Mechanical Dissection

As part of MAE 3230, my team selected a gas-powered chainsaw and produced an instructional video explaining its fluid-dynamic behavior. I led the mechanical teardown and focused my analysis on the rotating flow generated by the flywheel.

From a fluid-mechanics standpoint, the flywheel blades act like a centrifugal impeller, producing outward airflow that helps cool the engine. This project helped me connect course concepts such as mass flow, velocity changes, pressure rise, and forced convection to a real mechanical system.

MAE 3780 — Robot Project

Autonomous Cube-Collecting Robot

For my MAE 3780 Robot Project, my team designed, built, and programmed an autonomous robot to collect cubes placed in the center of a competition field. Our robot used a front-door collection mechanism, side extensions, QTI sensors, DC motors, and a servo-controlled door system to collect cubes while staying inside the black border.

I contributed to the mechanical design and integration of the collection system. I helped build and test the front-door mechanism, which used thin wood doors, torsional springs, and a servo-controlled string system to open during the run. I also helped adjust the wiring, sensor response, and strategy so the mechanical design, electronics, and code worked together during competition.

Heat Transfer

Problem Set 3 Reflection

For my Heat Transfer portfolio assignment, I chose Problem Set 3 because it helped me connect several major course topics to realistic engineering systems. The assignment covered transient and steady heat conduction, internal heat generation, composite walls, convection boundary conditions, and thermal resistance networks.

This problem set was valuable because it made me practice moving from a physical system to an energy balance, then to equations and engineering conclusions. The turbine blade thermal barrier coating problem especially showed me how heat transfer analysis is used in design decisions where overheating can affect material failure and reliability.
